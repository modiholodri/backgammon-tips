#### When You're Ahead

If you're leading the race, your number one objective is efficiency. 
You've already done the hard work of getting your checkers home first, so now it's all about maintaining control and finishing cleanly.
Think of it as a race where you're just ahead of your rival - you don't need to sprint recklessly;
you just need to keep your rhythm smooth and steady to cross the finish line first.

- Keep your checkers spread out.
- Bear off from the highest points first (to avoid wastage later).
- Don't take unnecessary risks.

Also, don't be afraid to double if you're clearly in front. 
Many players miss out on potential points because they hesitate to use the cube aggressively when the math is in their favour.
Sometimes it's better to take a risk and just go for it as opposed to holding back.
