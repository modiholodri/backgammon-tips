#### The Art of Hitting: Slotting and Building

Slotting means leaving a blot on a point you want to make, betting that you can cover it next turn. Slot when the target point is valuable enough to justify the risk. Avoid slotting when the opponent has a strong home board, because getting hit against a board with four or five made points is devastating.

Note the difference between slotting and splitting.
- Slotting places a blot on a point you want to make (usually in your own home board or outer board).
- Splitting moves one of your back checkers to fight for an anchor in the opponent's board. Both leave blots, but they serve different purposes.
